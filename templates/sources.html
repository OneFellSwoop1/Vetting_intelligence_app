Our Data Sources

Comprehensive information about the lobbying and contract data integrated in our platform.

The Vetting Intelligence Hub integrates data from multiple government sources to provide a comprehensive view of lobbying activities and government contracts. Below you'll find detailed information about each of our data sources, including what's included, how often it's updated, and any limitations you should be aware of.

Federal Data Sources

Senate Lobbying Disclosure Act Database

Federal
Data Source
U.S. Senate Office of Public Records
Website
https://lda.senate.gov/system/public/
Update Frequency
Quarterly (with some delay in publishing)
Data Available From
1999 to present

Overview

The Senate Lobbying Disclosure Act (LDA) database contains all lobbying registrations and reports filed with the Secretary of the Senate as required by the Lobbying Disclosure Act of 1995 (as amended). This database is the most comprehensive source of federal lobbying activity in the United States.

What's Included

  • Lobbying firm registrations
  • Quarterly reports of lobbying activities
  • Information on clients, lobbyists, and issues
  • Reported income/expenses for lobbying activities
  • Government entities contacted
  • Specific lobbying issues and bill numbers (when disclosed)

Limitations

  • Relies on self-reporting by lobbying firms and organizations
  • Only includes activities that meet the statutory threshold for disclosure
  • Does not include grassroots lobbying
  • Limited details on specific lobbying activities and contacts
  • Quarterly filing schedule means data can be up to 3-4 months delayed

New York City Data Sources

NYC Lobbying Bureau Database

Local
Data Source
New York City Clerk's Office, Lobbying Bureau
Website
https://www1.nyc.gov/site/clerk/lobbying/lobbying-bureau.page
Update Frequency
Bimonthly reports, with annual registrations
Data Available From
2006 to present

Overview

The NYC Lobbying Bureau database contains all lobbying activity reported to the City Clerk's Lobbying Bureau as required by the NYC Lobbying Law. This database provides detailed information about lobbying activities targeting New York City government officials and agencies.

What's Included

  • Annual lobbying registrations
  • Bimonthly periodic reports
  • Information on clients, lobbyists, and targets
  • Specific subjects and matters lobbied on
  • NYC agencies contacted
  • Compensation and reimbursed expenses

Limitations

  • Self-reported data with varying levels of detail
  • Limited information on specific meetings or contacts
  • Only covers lobbying of NYC government entities
  • Thresholds for reporting may exclude some activities

NYC Checkbook (Contract Data)

Local
Data Source
New York City Open Data / Checkbook NYC
Website
https://www.checkbooknyc.com/
Update Frequency
Daily to weekly (varies by dataset)
Data Available From
Fiscal Year 2011 to present

Overview

Checkbook NYC provides detailed information on New York City's financial transactions, including contracts, spending, and payroll. Our platform specifically integrates contract data to help identify relationships between vendors and city agencies.

What's Included

  • Active expense contracts
  • Contract registration details
  • Maximum contract amounts
  • Contract start and end dates
  • Contracting agencies
  • Vendor information
  • Contract purposes and categories

Limitations

  • Limited historical data before 2011
  • Variations in data quality and completeness across agencies
  • Limited details on contract performance metrics
  • Some contracts may have redacted information for security or privacy reasons
