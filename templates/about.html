About the Vetting Intelligence Hub

A comprehensive platform for lobbying disclosure data transparency and ethical decision-making.

Our Mission

The Vetting Intelligence Hub was created to make lobbying data more accessible, transparent, and actionable. By integrating multiple data sources into a single platform, we aim to provide journalists, researchers, ethics officers, and concerned citizens with the tools they need to investigate conflicts of interest, monitor influence in government, and make more informed decisions.

What We Do

Our platform brings together lobbying disclosure data from federal, state, and local sources, enabling users to:

  • Search across multiple lobbying disclosure databases with a unified interface
  • Identify potential conflicts of interest or overlapping relationships
  • Visualize lobbying trends over time and across entities
  • Investigate specific individuals, organizations, or government agencies
  • Export data for further research and analysis

Our Data Sources

The Vetting Intelligence Hub currently integrates data from the following sources:

  • Federal Level: Senate Lobbying Disclosure Act (LDA) database
  • Local Level: New York City Lobbying Bureau data
  • Local Level: NYC Checkbook (contracts and spending data)

We are continually working to add more data sources and enhance our existing integrations to provide the most comprehensive view possible of lobbying and government influence.

Why It Matters

Transparency in lobbying and government influence is essential for maintaining public trust and ensuring ethical governance. By making this data easily accessible and providing tools to analyze it effectively, we aim to:

  • Prevent conflicts of interest before they arise
  • Hold power accountable through data-driven journalism and research
  • Enhance public trust through transparent decision-making
  • Empower citizens with information about how lobbying affects governance
